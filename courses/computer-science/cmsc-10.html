COMPUTER SCIENCE

Course Credits: 3 Units

Prerequisites: None

CMSC 10 - Introduction to Computer Science

This is a core/major course to introduce students to software systems and methodologies in software development, computer theory, and computer organization and architecture integrating relevant social and ethical issues related to computer science. CMSC 10 is a prerequisite to CMSC 28.

Course Description

Introduction to the major areas of computer science – software systems and methodology, computer theory, computer organization and architecture – and relevant social and ethical issues.

Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of the semester, students would have been introduced to the basic concepts of computer science. Specifically, the students would be able to:

  1. Design algorithms for a variety of problems;
  2. Explain the theoretical and practical limitations of computers;
  3. Describe the structure and organization of computers;
  4. Explain the importance of systems software in the execution of programs;
  5. Discuss major computer applications; and
  6. Discuss relevant social and ethical issues.
Course Outline

I. Introduction (Nature of Computer Science) (1)

II. Elements of Programming (4)

  1. Design of Algorithm
  2. Writing Algorithms
  3. Implementation of Algorithms
  4. Techniques in Program Development

III. Theory of Algorithms (2)

  1. Program Correctness
  2. Complexity of Algorithms
  3. Computability
